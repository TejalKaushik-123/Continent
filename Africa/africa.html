The Africa

Africa, the second-largest continent on Earth, covers an area of about 30.37 million square kilometers, which accounts for approximately 20% of the world's total land area. It is home to 54 recognized countries and boasts immense geographic, cultural, and economic diversity.

Among Africa’s most powerful and influential countries are Nigeria, South Africa, and Egypt. Nigeria, the most populous country, has a large and growing economy driven by oil and gas. South Africa stands out for its advanced infrastructure, industrial base, and regional leadership. Egypt, strategically located, holds political and cultural significance with a strong economy rooted in tourism, agriculture, and the Suez Canal.

Africa is incredibly rich in natural resources, including vast reserves of oil, natural gas, gold, diamonds, cobalt, and platinum. The Democratic Republic of Congo, for example, holds over 60% of the world’s cobalt—critical for modern electronics and batteries.

The largest country by area in Africa is Algeria, covering over 2.38 million square kilometers. Despite its arid deserts, it possesses significant oil and gas reserves, contributing heavily to its economy.

Africa’s natural wealth and strategic importance continue to attract global interest, positioning it as a continent of immense potential and influence.

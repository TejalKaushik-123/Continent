Asia

Asia, the largest and most populous continent, is home to some of the world’s most powerful countries. Dominating the region are economic giants like China, the largest country by both population and area, covering about 9.6 million square kilometers, which accounts for approximately 30% of Asia's total land area. It’s rich in resources, with vast deposits of coal, rare earth metals, and fertile agricultural land. India, another powerhouse, is second in population and also a major economic force, with a rapidly growing tech sector and a diverse economy that ranges from agriculture to space exploration.

In terms of land area, Russia—though primarily considered a part of both Asia and Europe—spans a staggering 17.1 million square kilometers, about 11% of global land area, making it the largest country in the world. Southeast Asia, with its rich biodiversity, is home to the Philippines and Indonesia, countries rich in natural resources like timber, oil, and gas.

Asia’s significance goes beyond land and resources. It’s the cradle of ancient civilizations and continues to play a central role in global politics and trade. From the industrial powerhouses of China and Japan to the emerging economies in South Asia, Asia’s influence is undeniable and ever-expanding.

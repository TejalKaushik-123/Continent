Australia

Australia, often referred to as Oceania when including surrounding islands and countries, is the smallest continent by land area, covering approximately 8.6 million square kilometers. This represents about 5.8% of Earth’s total land surface. Despite its size, the continent holds significant global importance due to its resources and strategic location.

The most powerful and dominant country in the continent is Australia, both in terms of landmass and economic strength. It occupies the majority of the continent and is one of the world’s most developed nations, with strong institutions, a stable government, and a high standard of living.

Australia is rich in natural resources, including iron ore, coal, gold, natural gas, uranium, and bauxite. It is one of the world’s top exporters of these minerals, especially iron ore and coal, which play a key role in the global energy and manufacturing sectors. Agriculture is also vital, with large exports of wool, wheat, and beef.

Other countries in the Oceania region include New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, and several Pacific island nations, but they are smaller and less influential globally

Australia's wealth of resources, political stability, and economic power make it the cornerstone of the Oceania region and a key player in global trade.

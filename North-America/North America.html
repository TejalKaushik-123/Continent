North America

North America is home to some of the world’s most powerful and influential countries. Among them are the United States, Canada, and Mexico. The United States is the largest economy in the region and a global leader in finance, technology, and military strength. Canada, rich in natural resources like oil, minerals, and timber, boasts one of the world’s highest standards of living. Mexico, with its vibrant culture and significant manufacturing and agricultural industries, is also an economic powerhouse in the region.

In terms of geographical size, Canada is the largest country in North America, covering approximately 9.98 million square kilometers. It accounts for roughly 16.4% of the continent's total land area. The United States follows closely, with a landmass of around 9.83 million square kilometers, making it the third-largest country in the world by area. Mexico ranks third in North America, spanning around 1.96 million square kilometers.

North America as a whole covers about 24.7 million square kilometers, or 16.5% of the Earth's total land area. It is rich in diverse ecosystems, from the Arctic tundra in Canada to the deserts of Mexico, and abundant natural resources such as fossil fuels, timber, and freshwater. This combination of vast land, resources, and economic might makes North America a crucial player on the global stage.
